# Financial Planning — Deep Dive Reference

> ⚠️ Work with a qualified accountant or financial advisor for accurate projections.

---

## Startup Cost Categories

### One-Time Setup Costs
- Legal & registration fees
- Branding (logo, website design)
- Equipment, tools, or machinery
- Initial inventory or raw materials
- Office setup / lease deposit
- Software licenses and tools

### Recurring Monthly Costs (Operating Expenses)
- Rent / co-working space
- Salaries and contractor fees
- Marketing and advertising budget
- Software subscriptions (SaaS tools)
- Insurance premiums
- Utilities and logistics
- Loan repayments (if applicable)

---

## Budget Planning by Stage

### Pre-Launch (Month -3 to 0)
Focus: Validation, legal, brand, MVP
- Keep costs minimal — validate before spending
- Target: spend only what's needed to get first paying customer

### Launch (Month 0–3)
Focus: Customer acquisition, operations setup
- Marketing spend begins
- Hire minimum viable team

### Growth (Month 3–12)
Focus: Scale what's working, cut what isn't
- Reinvest revenue into highest-ROI channels
- Build cash reserves (3–6 months runway target)

## Break-Even Analysis Framework

```
Break-Even Point = Fixed Monthly Costs ÷ Gross Margin per Unit

Example:
- Fixed costs: $5,000/month
- Product price: $100
- Cost per unit: $40
- Gross margin: $60/unit
- Break-even: $5,000 ÷ $60 = 84 units/month
```

## Funding Options

| Source | Amount Range | Pros | Cons |
|--------|-------------|------|------|
| Bootstrapping | Any | Full control | Slow growth |
| Friends & Family | $1K–$50K | Easy terms | Relationship risk |
| Bank Loan | $10K–$500K | Retain equity | Requires collateral |
| Angel Investor | $25K–$500K | Mentorship | Equity dilution |
| Venture Capital | $500K+ | Scale fast | High pressure, major dilution |

| Revenue-Based Financing | $10K–$5M | Flexible repayment | Higher total cost |

## Key Financial Metrics to Track

| Metric | What It Means | Target |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| Burn Rate | Monthly cash spent | Know your runway |
| Runway | Months until cash runs out | 12–18 months minimum |
| CAC | Cost to acquire one customer | CAC < LTV/3 |
| LTV | Revenue per customer lifetime | Maximize |
| Gross Margin | Revenue minus direct costs | 40%+ for services, 20%+ for products |
| MRR/ARR | Monthly/Annual Recurring Revenue | Track growth rate |
| Churn Rate | % customers lost per month | <5% for SaaS, <2% for subscriptions |
| Payback Period | Months to recover CAC | Under 12 months ideally |

---

## Cash Flow Warning Signs
- Revenue is growing but cash is shrinking (check receivables)
- Gross margin below 20% — hard to survive
- CAC exceeding LTV — unsustainable acquisition
- Over-reliance on one client (>30% of revenue)
- No cash reserve for 2+ months of operations
- Delayed payments from customers piling up

## Pricing Strategy Frameworks

### Cost-Plus Pricing
Price = Total cost per unit × (1 + desired margin %)
- Simple, ensures profitability
- Risk: may underprice or overprice vs. market

### Value-Based Pricing
Price = What the customer is willing to pay based on perceived value
- Higher margins possible
- Requires strong positioning and research

### Competitive Pricing
Price relative to competitors (at, above, or below)
- Safe for commoditized markets
- Risky if you're not the cost leader

### Penetration Pricing
Launch low → build market share → raise prices
- Good for new entrants in competitive markets
- Risk: attracts price-sensitive customers, hard to raise later

### Premium / Skimming
Launch high → target early adopters → lower over time
- Works for innovative or luxury products
- Requires strong brand and differentiation
